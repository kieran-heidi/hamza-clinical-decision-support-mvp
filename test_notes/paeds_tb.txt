### Patient Information
- **Patient:** Sarah K.
- **DOB:** 11/03/2020
- **Age:** 5 years
- **Weight:** 15 kg (Note: Documented **poor weight gain** on growth chart)

### Presenting Complaint
- Sarah presents with a **non-remitting cough** lasting **six weeks** that is not improving with standard antibiotics.
- Parents report **intermittent low-grade fever** (mostly evening) and **decreased energy/playfulness** over the same period.
- History of **contact** with a household member (grandfather) recently diagnosed with smear-positive pulmonary TB.

### History
- **Onset:** Chronic, non-specific symptoms began approximately 6 weeks ago.
- **Progression:** Cough is mostly dry, persistent, and has not responded to a 7-day course of Amoxicillin.
- **Constitutional Symptoms:** Intermittent evening temperature up to $37.8^\circ\text{C}$. Noted **weight loss** (or failure to gain weight) over the last 2 months. Reports occasional night sweats.
- **Exposure:** Known close household contact with an adult who has confirmed **infectious PTB**.
- **Past Medical History:** Unremarkable. No history of chronic respiratory illness.
- **Immunization/Development:** Fully vaccinated, including BCG scar present. Developmentally appropriate for age.
- **Allergies:** No known drug allergies.

### Examination
- **General:** Alert, cooperative, but appears **mildly thin** and **lethargic**.
- **Vitals:** RR 24, HR 98, SpO2 98% on room air, T $37.4^\circ\text{C}$.
- **Respiratory:** Chest clear to auscultation; no wheeze, crackles, or signs of acute respiratory distress.
- **ENT:** Oropharynx clear.
- **CVS:** Normal.
- **Abdomen:** Soft, non-tender. No hepatosplenomegaly.
- **Lymphatics:** No significant peripheral lymphadenopathy (LAD) noted.
- **Growth:** Weight $15$ kg (below 10th percentile, confirmed poor weight trajectory).

### Assessment
- Sarah presents with symptoms (chronic cough, low-grade fever, poor weight gain) and an **epidemiological link** highly suggestive of **uncomplicated primary pulmonary Tuberculosis (PTB)**.
- The clinical presentation is **non-severe** (no severe respiratory distress). The disease is likely **paucibacillary**.
- **Plan to investigate** with Tuberculin Skin Test (TST)/Interferon Gamma Release Assay (IGRA) and Chest X-Ray (CXR) to support clinical diagnosis.

### Plan
- Plan as per local guidelines for childhood tuberculosis.
