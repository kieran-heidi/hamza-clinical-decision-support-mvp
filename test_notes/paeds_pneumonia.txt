### Patient Information
- **Patient:** Ethan B.
- **DOB:** 10/15/2020
- **Age:** 5 years
- **Weight:** 15 kg

### Presenting Complaint
- Ethan presented with a **4-day history of cough and fever** that has worsened in the last 24 hours.
- The cough is now **productive** and he has experienced **difficulty breathing** and is tiring easily.
- Parents report a sustained high fever despite antipyretics.

### History
- **Onset:** Started 4 days ago with general malaise, lethargy, and a non-specific cough.
- **Progression:** Fever (up to $39.5^\circ\text{C}$) became more sustained. Cough is now deep and associated with occasional pleuritic-type chest pain.
- **Respiratory Symptoms:** Reports feeling short of breath, and parents noted **tachypnoea** (fast breathing) and decreased appetite.
- **Feeding/Fluid Intake:** Reduced fluid intake but no signs of severe dehydration.
- **Past Medical History:** No history of asthma, recurrent infections, or chronic respiratory disease.
- **Immunization/Development:** Fully vaccinated and developmentally appropriate for age.
- **Allergies:** No known drug allergies.

### Examination
- **General:** Alert but **moderately unwell**, lying still, reluctant to move, flushed appearance.
- **Vitals:** RR 40 (mild tachypnoea for age), HR 110, SpO2 94% on room air, T $38.9^\circ\text{C}$.
- **Respiratory:** Mild retractions (intercostal), but no accessory muscle use or nasal flaring. **Focal coarse crackles** and **bronchial breath sounds** noted over the right lower lung field. Percussion is dull over the same area. No significant wheeze.
- **ENT:** Mild erythema of the throat, no tonsillar exudate.
- **CVS:** Normal S1/S2, capillary refill time < 3 seconds.
- **Hydration:** Adequate.

### Assessment
- Ethan presents with clinical features highly suggestive of **community-acquired pneumonia (CAP)**, likely bacterial in origin.
- He has mild hypoxia (SpO2 94%) and tachypnoea, but is **not in severe respiratory distress** and is tolerating oral intake.
- Candidate for **outpatient management** with close follow-up.
- No signs suggestive of complications (e.g., empyema, sepsis).

### Plan
- Plan as per local guidelines for community-acquired pneumonia in children.
